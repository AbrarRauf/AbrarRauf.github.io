About Me

Hi, my name is Abrar Rauf. I recently received my undergraduate degree from the department of Materials & Metallurgical Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering & technology. Over the last year of my undergrad, I was heavily involved with several computational research projects in the field of photovoltaics and energy storage. These projects were carried out through collaboration with the research group of Dr. Saquib Ahmed from SUNY Buffalo University. I modelled & investigated thin film perovskite solar devices through numerical simulation, density functional theory & machine learning. I also collaborated on a molecular dynamics simulation of bilayer silicene anode with Mg-ion intercalation. My work on these projects was driven by a passion for exploring the intersection between material science & renewable energy. I wish to continue to investigate these material systems & expand my research through experimental synthesis & characterization techniques during my post graduate studies. I have a keen interest in exploring stability and degradation of these types of materials/devices and work towards removing barriers to their commercialization. I want to dig deeper into these material systems and phenomena through first principles/informatics driven computational studies, along with experimental synthesis and characterization.

Projects Overview

I have a keen interest in understanding the electronic & atomistic structures of a material & correlating its properties & device scale performance. The research I have been involved with has touched upon the following three areas of study:

1. Numerical Simulation of Solar Cells.

  • Simulation of realistic thin film perovskite solar devices
  • Optimization of absorber layer thickness
  • Evaluating the impact of defect concentration & defect energy levels
  • Exploring novel tandem configurations for maximum power conversion efficiency

    • 2. Molecular Dynamics Simulation of Mg-ion Silicene Battery.

  • Atomistic modeling of buckled bi-layer silicene
  • Structure equilibration using appropriate ensembles
  • Evaluating Cohesive & Adsorption energies
  • Exploring Mg-ion intercalation with MSD & RDF
  • Studying effect of electric field on ion migration

    • 3. Density Functional Theory

  • Modeling the lattices of inorganic & organic Sn-based perovskites
  • Implementing schemes for increasing the accuracy of band gap prediction
  • Studying the impact of perovskite stoichiometry on Lattice & electronic density of states
  • Extending DFT predictions to device scale performance through machine learning
